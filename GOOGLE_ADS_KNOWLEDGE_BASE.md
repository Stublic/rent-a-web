## 1. The Anatomy of a Perfect Ad

### 1.1 Campaign Types We Generate For

| Feature | Search Campaign | Performance Max (PMax) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Where ads show** | Google Search results only | All Google surfaces (Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Maps, Discover) |
| **Targeting** | Keyword-based | AI-driven (audience signals + assets) |
| **Assets needed** | Headlines + Descriptions | Headlines + Long Headlines + Descriptions + Images + Videos |
| **Best for** | High-intent keyword queries | Broad reach across all channels |

### 1.2 Text Asset Specifications

These are **hard limits** enforced by Google. The AI must **never** exceed them.

| Asset Type | Max Characters | Quantity Required (PMax) | Quantity Required (Search) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Short Headline** | **30** | Up to 15 (aim for 15) | Up to 15 (aim for 15) |
| **Long Headline** | **90** | Up to 5 (aim for at least 4) | N/A |
| **Short Description** | **60** | 1 (mandatory first description) | N/A |
| **Description** | **90** | Up to 4 additional (aim for 4) | Up to 4 (aim for 4) |
| **Business Name** | **25** | 1 | N/A |
| **Display Path** | **15** each (2 fields) | 1 set | 1 set |
| **Call to Action** | Predefined list | 1 | N/A |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Character counting rules:**
> - Spaces count as characters.
> - Special characters (é, ü, ñ, č, ž, š, etc.) each count as **one** character.
> - The AI must validate every asset against these limits **before** returning results.
> - When generating in Croatian or other languages with diacritics, pay special attention to word length.

### 1.3 Search Ad Structure (Responsive Search Ads — RSA)

A single RSA can contain:
- **Up to 15 headlines** (30 chars each) — at least 3 will show at a time.
- **Up to 4 descriptions** (90 chars each) — at least 2 will show at a time.
- Google's AI mixes and matches the best-performing combinations.

### 1.4 PMax Asset Group Structure

A single PMax Asset Group can contain:
- **Up to 15 short headlines** (30 chars each)
- **Up to 5 long headlines** (90 chars each)
- **1 short description** (60 chars)
- **Up to 4 descriptions** (90 chars each)
- Images, videos, logos (handled separately — not generated by our AI text model)
- A **Final URL** (the user's website)

### 1.5 Ad Strength Target

Google rates every ad/asset group on a scale: **Poor → Average → Good → Excellent**.

**Our target is always "Excellent."** To achieve this:
- Maximize the number of unique assets (fill every slot).
- Ensure high diversity across assets (see Section 3).
- Include keywords/search themes naturally in headlines.

## 2. Copywriting Rules

### 2.1 The Golden Rules of Ad Copy

| Rule # | Rule | ✅ Good Example | ❌ Bad Example |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Lead with a benefit, not a feature** | "Save 50% On Energy Bills" | "We Sell Solar Panels" |
| 2 | **Include a clear Call to Action (CTA)** | "Book Your Free Consultation" | "We Are Available" |
| 3 | **Be specific — use numbers and data** | "Trusted by 500+ Families" | "Many People Trust Us" |
| 4 | **Create urgency (when appropriate)** | "Limited Spots — Call Today" | "Contact Us Sometime" |
| 5 | **Address the customer directly (use "You/Your")** | "Get Your Dream Kitchen" | "We Build Kitchens" |
| 6 | **Match user search intent** | "Emergency Plumber Near You" | "ABC Plumbing Services" |
| 7 | **Localize the copy** | "Best Hair Salon in Zagreb" | "Best Hair Salon" |

### 2.2 Capitalization Rules

- **Use Title Case** for headlines: capitalize the first letter of each significant word.
  - ✅ `Professional Roof Repair in Split`
  - ❌ `professional roof repair in split`
  - ❌ `PROFESSIONAL ROOF REPAIR IN SPLIT` (all caps = shouting = policy violation)
- **Use Sentence case** for descriptions: capitalize only the first word and proper nouns.
  - ✅ `Our certified technicians will fix your roof in 24 hours. Call now for a free estimate.`
  - ❌ `Our Certified Technicians Will Fix Your Roof In 24 Hours.`
- **Never** use ALL CAPS for entire words (except established acronyms like "HVAC" or "AC").

### 2.3 Punctuation Rules

| Rule | ✅ Allowed | ❌ Prohibited |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Exclamation marks | Max **one** per asset | "Buy Now!!!" or "Best Deal!!" |
| Question marks | Allowed in headlines for engagement | Excessive: "Need Help??? Call Now???" |
| Ellipsis (...) | Avoid entirely | "The best deal..." |
| Ampersand (&) | Allowed to save characters | N/A |
| Symbols (★, ♦, ►) | **Never** use decorative symbols | "★★★ Best Plumber ★★★" |
| Emojis | **Never** use emojis | "🔥 Hot Deal 🔥" |

### 2.4 Prohibited Content & Policy Compliance

The AI must **never** generate:

- **Superlative claims without proof:** "The Best Plumber in Croatia" (unless backed by a verifiable award). Use "Top-Rated" or "Highly Rated" instead.
- **Misleading promises:** "100% Guaranteed Results" — be honest and realistic.
- **Trademark violations:** Do not mention competitor brand names.
- **Clickbait language:** "You Won't Believe This Price!" — maintain professional tone.
- **Excessive repetition of the business name:** The brand name should appear in 2–3 headlines max, not all 15.
- **Phone numbers in ad text:** Google has Call Assets for this; putting numbers in headlines/descriptions can trigger disapproval.
- **Price claims that can't be verified:** "Cheapest in Town" without proof.

### 2.5 Writing Style Guidelines

- **Tone:** Professional, confident, approachable. Never aggressive or desperate.
- **Language level:** Simple, clear language. Assume the reader is a busy consumer, not an industry expert.
- **Sentence length (descriptions):** Short, punchy sentences. 1–2 sentences per description.
- **Avoid jargon:** Write "Fix your leaky pipes" not "Comprehensive hydraulic infrastructure remediation."
- **Power words to use:** Free, Save, Fast, Trusted, Certified, Professional, Expert, Near You, Today, Now, Custom, Affordable, Quality, Guaranteed, Local.

## 3. Asset Diversity (PMax Specific)

### 3.1 Why Diversity Matters

Google's AI tests **thousands** of combinations of your headlines, descriptions, and images. If your assets are repetitive:
- Google has fewer meaningful combinations to test → worse optimization.
- Ad strength drops to "Poor" or "Average" → less ad inventory.
- Your ads look repetitive to users → lower Click-Through Rate (CTR).

> [!CAUTION]
> **The #1 mistake:** Writing 15 variations of the same headline. "Best Plumber Zagreb," "Top Plumber Zagreb," "Great Plumber Zagreb" — this is **NOT diversity**. Each headline must present a **different angle**.

### 3.2 The 5-Angle Framework

Every set of assets must cover these **5 angles**. The AI should distribute headlines across them:

| Angle | Purpose | Headline Example (30 chars) | Description Example (90 chars) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 🏷️ **Brand** | Build trust and recognition | `Petar's Plumbing — Est. 2005` | `Family-owned plumbing business serving Zagreb for over 18 years. Licensed and insured.` |
| 💡 **Benefit** | Answer "What's in it for me?" | `Save Up to 30% on Repairs` | `Our efficient repair methods save you money. No hidden fees, no surprises on your bill.` |
| 🔧 **Feature** | Highlight what you offer | `24/7 Emergency Service` | `We're available around the clock for burst pipes, leaks, and drainage emergencies.` |
| ⏰ **Urgency** | Create a reason to act now | `Book Today — Limited Slots` | `Don't wait for a small leak to become a big problem. Schedule your inspection today.` |
| 📞 **CTA (Call to Action)** | Tell the user what to do | `Get a Free Quote Now` | `Request your free, no-obligation quote online in just 2 minutes. Fast response guaranteed.` |

### 3.3 Recommended Distribution for 15 Short Headlines

| Angle | Number of Headlines | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 🏷️ Brand | 2–3 | Include business name, years in business, locality |
| 💡 Benefit | 3–4 | Focus on money saved, time saved, quality of life improvement |
| 🔧 Feature | 3–4 | Specific services, certifications, unique selling propositions |
| ⏰ Urgency | 2–3 | Seasonal offers, limited availability, time-sensitive language |
| 📞 CTA | 2–3 | Different actions: call, book online, get a quote, visit |

### 3.4 Recommended Distribution for 5 Long Headlines

| # | Angle | Example (90 chars) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Brand + Location | `Petar's Plumbing — Zagreb's Most Trusted Plumber for Residential and Commercial Jobs` |
| 2 | Benefit-Driven | `Fix Your Plumbing Problems Fast — Save Money with Our Transparent No-Surprise Pricing` |
| 3 | Feature-Focused | `Licensed and Insured Plumber Offering 24/7 Emergency Drain, Pipe, and Boiler Repair` |
| 4 | Urgency/Seasonal | `Winter Is Coming — Book Your Boiler Service Now Before Appointments Fill Up` |
| 5 | CTA-Driven | `Get Your Free Plumbing Estimate in Minutes — Book Online or Call Us Today` |

### 3.5 Recommended Distribution for Descriptions

| # | Type | Char Limit | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Short Description | 60 | Concise elevator pitch of the business |
| 2 | Description | 90 | Expand on the main benefit (save money, better results) |
| 3 | Description | 90 | Highlight trust signals (years of experience, reviews, certifications) |
| 4 | Description | 90 | Detail a specific key service or feature |
| 5 | Description | 90 | Strong CTA with an incentive (free quote, discount, easy booking) |

### 3.6 Diversity Validation Checklist

The AI must self-check every asset set against this list before returning results:

- [ ] No two headlines share more than 50% of the same words.
- [ ] All 5 angles (Brand, Benefit, Feature, Urgency, CTA) are represented.
- [ ] The business name appears in **no more than 3** short headlines.
- [ ] Location/city name appears in **at least 2** but **no more than 5** headlines.
- [ ] At least 2 headlines contain a clear CTA verb (Book, Call, Get, Request, Visit).
- [ ] At least 1 headline contains a number or data point (years, %, count).
- [ ] No headline is a subset or near-duplicate of another headline.
- [ ] Descriptions cover different topics — no two descriptions deliver the same message.

## 4. Keyword Strategy for Local Businesses

### 4.1 Search Intent Tiers

Our users (plumbers, hair salons, electricians, dentists, etc.) need keywords that capture people **ready to buy or book**. Organize keywords by intent:

| Tier | Intent Level | Pattern | Example Keywords |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 🔴 **Tier 1 — Hot** | Transactional / Emergency | `[service] + [action]` | "book plumber," "hire electrician," "emergency locksmith" |
| 🟠 **Tier 2 — Warm** | Local + Commercial Intent | `[service] + [location]` | "plumber Zagreb," "hair salon Split," "dentist Rijeka" |
| 🟡 **Tier 3 — Engaged** | Comparison / Research | `[service] + [qualifier]` | "best plumber near me," "affordable hair salon," "top-rated dentist" |
| 🟢 **Tier 4 — Broad** | Informational (use sparingly) | `[problem / question]` | "how to fix a leaky faucet," "best haircut for round face" |

> [!TIP]
> **For local businesses, 80% of the keyword budget should go to Tier 1 and Tier 2 keywords.** These people are ready to act. Tier 3 can complement. Tier 4 is generally not recommended for small budgets.

### 4.2 Keyword Generation Rules

When the AI generates keywords for a local business, follow these rules:

1. **Always include `[service] + [city/area]` combinations:**
   - "plumber Zagreb"
   - "plumber Trešnjevka" (neighborhood-level)
   - "plumber near me" (Google handles geolocation)

2. **Include service-specific long-tail keywords:**
   - "emergency pipe repair Zagreb"
   - "boiler installation Sesvete"
   - "clogged drain service Dubrava"

3. **Include intent-modified keywords:**
   - "hire [service]"
   - "book [service]"
   - "[service] open now"
   - "[service] prices"
   - "[service] reviews"
   - "best [service] near me"
   - "cheap [service] [location]"
   - "affordable [service] [location]"

4. **Include problem-based keywords:**
   - "burst pipe help"
   - "toilet won't flush repair"
   - "broken boiler fix"

5. **Include seasonal and urgency keywords (when relevant):**
   - "AC repair summer"
   - "boiler service before winter"
   - "holiday hair styling"

### 4.3 Negative Keywords (Essential for Cost Savings)

Always suggest a starter negative keyword list to prevent wasted ad spend:

| Category | Negative Keywords to Suggest |
| --- | --- |
| **DIY / Informational** | "how to," "DIY," "tutorial," "guide," "what is," "YouTube" |
| **Jobs / Careers** | "jobs," "hiring," "salary," "career," "vacancy," "internship" |
| **Education** | "course," "certification," "school," "training," "degree" |
| **Free / Unqualified** | "free" (if the business doesn't offer free services) |
| **Unrelated locations** | Cities/regions the business does NOT serve |
| **Competitor brands** | Known competitor names (avoid bidding on these for small budgets) |

### 4.4 Keyword Match Types (Reference)

When setting up Search campaigns, these match types matter:

| Match Type | Syntax | Behavior | When to Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Broad Match** | `plumber Zagreb` | Widest reach, AI-matched related queries | PMax default; Search only with Smart Bidding |
| **Phrase Match** | `"plumber Zagreb"` | Query must contain the phrase in order | Good balance of reach and control |
| **Exact Match** | `[plumber Zagreb]` | Query must match the meaning exactly | Highest control, use for top-converting terms |

> [!NOTE]
> For Performance Max campaigns, keywords work differently — they are called **Search Themes** and function similarly to broad match signals. The AI should suggest 10–25 search themes per asset group, based on the keyword tiers above.

### 4.5 Keywords-to-Ad-Copy Alignment

The AI must ensure that the **top 3–5 keywords** naturally appear in the generated headlines and descriptions. This is critical because:

- **Quality Score improves** when ad text matches the search query.
- **Ad Rank increases**, meaning lower cost-per-click (CPC).
- **User experience improves** — the user sees their search query reflected in the ad.

**Rule:** At least **5 of the 15 short headlines** should contain one of the primary target keywords or a close variant.

## 5. Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

### Asset Generation Template (for AI Prompt Engineering)

```
BUSINESS_NAME: [from user input]
BUSINESS_TYPE: [from user input, e.g., "plumber"]
CITY: [from user input, e.g., "Zagreb"]
SERVICES: [from user input, e.g., "pipe repair, boiler installation, drain cleaning"]
USP: [from user input, e.g., "24/7 service, 15 years experience, free quotes"]

GENERATE:
- 15 short headlines (max 30 chars each, covering all 5 angles)
- 5 long headlines (max 90 chars each, each a different angle)
- 1 short description (max 60 chars)
- 4 descriptions (max 90 chars each, each covering a different topic)
- 25 keywords (distributed across Tier 1, 2, and 3)
- 10 negative keywords
- 10 search themes (for PMax)

VALIDATE:
- All character limits respected
- Diversity checklist passed (Section 3.6)
- No policy violations (Section 2.4)
- Keywords appear naturally in at least 5 headlines
```

| Long Headline | **90 characters** |
